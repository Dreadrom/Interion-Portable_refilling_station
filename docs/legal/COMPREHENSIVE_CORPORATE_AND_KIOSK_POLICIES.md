# Comprehensive Corporate & Kiosk Operational Policies

Last updated: May 26, 2026

## Purpose

This document consolidates public-facing policy language for BlueDiesel Malaysia's B2B AdBlue supply business and kiosk-based dispensing operations, including the portable refill app.

This draft is written to align with common Malaysian legal and operational requirements, including the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), Contracts Act 1950, Consumer Protection Act 1999, Electronic Commerce Act 2006, and standard payment-network dispute handling practices. It is not a substitute for legal advice. A Malaysian-qualified lawyer should review this document before public release or incorporation into production terms.

## Recommended Publishing Structure

- Use the return and refund section inside the app before payment confirmation.
- Use the kiosk terms in the app account sign-up flow and at the station UI.
- Use the privacy policy as the public privacy notice linked from the app store listing and app settings.
- Keep a version number and acceptance timestamp for every user who accepts these terms.

## Public-Facing Policy Copy

### 1. Return and Refund Policy

#### B2B Operations

All volume-based fleet, logistics, industrial, and government deliveries are treated as final once delivery is completed and accepted at the delivery site, including by signed delivery order, electronic acknowledgment, or other recorded custody transfer.

Because every batch is supplied as ISO 22241-compliant AdBlue at the point of transfer, BlueDiesel does not accept returns of opened, unsealed, partly used, tampered, or contaminated storage units, including IBC totes and drums, due to chemical purity and contamination risks.

Where required by applicable law, or where BlueDiesel verifies a documented delivery shortfall, wrong product, or contamination attributable to BlueDiesel before the product is used, BlueDiesel may, at its option, replace the product, issue a credit note, or provide another legally appropriate remedy.

#### Kiosk and Retail Dispenser Operations

All AdBlue dispensed directly from a kiosk, pump, or automated dispensing nozzle into a vehicle tank or approved external container is final once the product exits the nozzle. For contamination-control reasons, dispensed product cannot be pumped back, extracted, or returned.

Customers are charged only for the actual volume successfully dispensed. If a payment hold, wallet hold, pre-authorization, or contactless authorization exceeds the final amount dispensed, the unused amount will be automatically released, reversed, or refunded to the original payment source or wallet balance, subject to bank, card-scheme, e-wallet, or payment-terminal processing timelines.

If a transaction fails, dispenses no product, or results in a duplicate or incorrect charge due to a verified system error, customers should contact BlueDiesel within 14 days of the transaction date. Verified billing errors will be corrected using the original payment channel, wallet credit, or another lawful remediation method.

This policy does not exclude any rights that cannot be excluded under applicable Malaysian law.

### 3. Terms and Conditions

#### 3.1 General Eligibility and Account Use

Users of the BlueDiesel kiosk platform, portable refill app, and related digital services must be at least 18 years old and legally capable of entering into binding agreements.

Customers must provide accurate, current, and complete registration, billing, and payment information and must keep that information updated.

BlueDiesel may refuse, suspend, limit, or terminate access to the app, kiosk, wallet, or dispensing services where it reasonably suspects fraud, misuse, unlawful conduct, tampering, chargeback abuse, false claims, unauthorized account sharing, excessive refund behavior, attempted system interference, or any act that puts safety, payment integrity, or product purity at risk.

#### 3.2 B2B Contractual Terms

Commercial agreements may be structured using volume-based pricing tiers, delivery schedules, account limits, and credit terms agreed in writing with the customer.

The customer must provide safe site access, suitable offloading conditions, compliant storage infrastructure, and any required personnel or safety escorts for delivery.

Risk in bulk product transfers to the customer upon custody transfer at the delivery point, subject to any non-excludable legal rights and any separately agreed written contract terms.

#### 3.3 Retail and Kiosk Dispensing Terms

AdBlue dispensed through kiosks is supplied on an as-available basis and subject to station operating status, hardware availability, payment authorization, communications availability, and safety interlocks. Except as required by applicable law, BlueDiesel does not provide additional warranties beyond those that cannot lawfully be excluded.

The customer is solely responsible for selecting the correct product, the correct fill point, and a suitable receiving container. AdBlue must never be introduced into a diesel fuel tank. BlueDiesel is not responsible for loss arising from customer misfueling, use of non-compliant containers, pre-existing vehicle defects, or customer mishandling after the product exits the nozzle.

Customers using an external container must use a clean, dedicated HDPE or stainless-steel container suitable for AdBlue. BlueDiesel may refuse or stop dispensing where a container appears unsafe, contaminated, unsuitable, or non-compliant.

Risk of spill, loss, misuse, degradation, or contamination passes to the customer when the product exits the nozzle, except to the extent caused by BlueDiesel's proven fraud, gross negligence, wilful misconduct, or other liability that cannot be excluded by law.

#### 3.4 Payments, Wallets, and Billing

The app may use wallet funding, pre-authorizations, payment holds, cards, e-wallets, QR payments, or other supported payment methods.

BlueDiesel may delay, reverse, reject, or cancel transactions where payment authorization fails, a pricing or system error is detected, a kiosk emergency stop is triggered, or abuse-prevention controls require review.

Refunds, reversals, and releases are processed according to the original payment channel and the relevant payment processor's operating rules. Banking and e-wallet clearing times may vary.

Customers must review transaction confirmations and report suspected unauthorized transactions promptly. BlueDiesel may require transaction details, receipts, account information, and supporting evidence before resolving a claim.

#### 3.5 Acceptable Use and Anti-Abuse Rules

Users must not:

- tamper with, damage, bypass, probe, or interfere with any kiosk, pump, QR code, payment terminal, meter, lock, valve, communications device, or safety system;
- use bots, scripts, emulators, rooted devices, reverse engineering tools, or unauthorized automation to access the app or kiosk workflow;
- create false accounts, share credentials, exploit promotions, repeatedly trigger dispute or refund flows in bad faith, or attempt to manipulate pricing, dispensing, or wallet balances;
- upload malicious code, scrape data, or attempt unauthorized access to any BlueDiesel system;
- use the services in a way that breaches any applicable law, regulation, or third-party right.

BlueDiesel may log device, session, transaction, station, and security-event data for safety, fraud prevention, dispute handling, and legal compliance.

#### 3.6 Limitation of Liability

To the fullest extent permitted by law, BlueDiesel's liability for kiosk or app claims is limited to the amount paid for the affected transaction or the minimum remedy required by law, whichever is higher.

Nothing in these terms limits liability for fraud, death or personal injury caused by negligence where liability cannot lawfully be excluded, wilful misconduct, or any consumer right or statutory guarantee that cannot be lawfully excluded or restricted.

#### 3.7 Governing Law

These terms are governed by the laws of Malaysia. Any dispute may be referred to the courts of Malaysia, subject to any mandatory dispute resolution or consumer rights available under applicable law.

### 4. Privacy Policy

#### 4.1 Who Controls Your Data

BlueDiesel Malaysia, including its operators of the portable refill app and kiosk services, acts as the data user for personal data collected through its app, website, customer support channels, and kiosk transactions.

#### 4.2 Personal Data We May Collect

Depending on how you use the services, BlueDiesel may collect:

- identity and account data, including name, mobile number, email address, company name, vehicle details, and account credentials;
- transaction and station data, including station ID, nozzle ID, volume dispensed, pricing, receipts, wallet activity, payment references, timestamps, and transaction outcomes;
- support and compliance data, including customer service communications, dispute records, incident reports, and verification records;

- create and manage accounts;
- authorize, secure, and complete dispensing transactions;

- investigate fraud, misuse, disputes, safety incidents, and chargebacks;
- provide customer support;
- comply with tax, accounting, audit, safety, and legal obligations;
- improve service performance, provided analytics and monitoring are used lawfully.

#### 4.6 Data Retention

Transaction and accounting records may be retained for at least 7 years where required for statutory, audit, or tax purposes.

#### 4.7 Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you may request access to your personal data, request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data, and withdraw consent for certain optional processing activities. Withdrawal of consent may affect BlueDiesel's ability to continue providing some services where the processing is necessary to operate your account or complete transactions.

#### 4.8 Security

BlueDiesel uses administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, misuse, or loss. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and BlueDiesel cannot guarantee absolute security.

#### 4.9 Cookies and Web Tracking

BlueDiesel's websites may use cookies and similar technologies to maintain sessions, remember preferences, improve usability, and generate aggregate analytics. Users can manage cookie preferences through browser controls or available consent tools where implemented.

## Internal Notes for Better App Protection

The policy language above helps, but contractual text alone will not protect the app against bad-faith users. Before launch, BlueDiesel should also implement the following operational controls:

- require an explicit terms and privacy acceptance checkbox and save the accepted version, timestamp, IP address, and device identifier;
- log each dispense event with station, nozzle, user ID, wallet ID, payment reference, meter start and end values, stop reason, and refund reason;
- expire QR sessions quickly and bind them to one authenticated session and one station;
- add rate limits for login, wallet top-up, cash-out, QR scan, and refund-triggering flows;
- add manual review rules for repeated partial-dispense refunds, repeated chargebacks, many accounts on one device, or abnormal cash-out behavior;
- require stronger identity verification before large wallet balances, repeated cash-outs, or suspicious activity;
- show an in-app confirmation that the user is responsible for selecting the correct fill point and approved container before dispensing starts;
- keep immutable audit records for disputes and payment reversals;
- set clear internal SOPs for emergency stop, non-dispense events, duplicate payments, contamination complaints, and refund investigations;
- ensure customer support responses do not promise refunds beyond the published policy unless approved by finance or management.
